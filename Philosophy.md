# Philosophy of the XPR Community Source License

## Knowledge Should Be Shared

Software is the one of the greatest educational tools ever created.

Source code allows people to study real systems, understand
engineering decisions, improve technical skills, and learn
through experimentation. 
Many developers became skilled not only from formal education, 
but by exploring the work of others, modifying projects, breaking and 
rebuilding things, and discovering how systems work.

The XPR Community Source License exists because learning
should remain accessible.

People should be able to:

* Study software
* Learn from real projects
* Experiment safely
* Modify systems
* Improve their skills
* Share knowledge with communities

Knowledge grows strongest when people can openly learn from
one another.

---

## Open Learning Does Not Mean Open Exploitation

Many creators want to share their work publicly without
turning it into unrestricted commercial property for others.

Software projects often represent:

* Years of learning
* Personal sacrifice
* Research and experimentation
* Creative problem-solving
* Maintenance and debugging
* Community support
* Long-term dedication

Traditional open-source licensing works well for many
projects, but not every creator wants unrestricted commercial
reuse of their work.

Some creators want:

* Public learning
* Community collaboration
* Visible source access
* Educational sharing

Without allowing:

* Unauthorized monetization
* Work stealing
* Misrepresentation
* Brand exploitation
* Commercial reselling by unrelated parties

XCSL exists to support that middle ground.

---

## Sustainable Sharing Matters

A healthy software ecosystem should support both:

* Community learning
* Creator sustainability

If creators lose all control over commercialization,
branding, or attribution, many eventually stop sharing
valuable work publicly.

XCSL encourages developers to share openly while still
allowing creators to:

* Maintain ownership
* Control commercial rights
* Protect project identity
* Prevent unfair exploitation
* Define project-specific rules

The goal is not to restrict communities.

The goal is to make public knowledge sharing sustainable.

---

## Source Availability Encourages Trust

Visible source code improves:

* Transparency
* Security review
* Educational value
* Community understanding
* Collaborative improvement

When publicly distributed modifications remain source-visible,
communities can continue learning from one another instead of
creating closed ecosystems built on previously open work.

XCSL encourages software communities where improvements remain
visible and educational for future developers.

---

## Independent Creativity Still Matters

XCSL does not claim ownership over ideas, concepts, genres,
or independently created software with similar functionality.

Innovation naturally builds upon previous inspiration.

People are free to:

* Create competing software
* Explore similar concepts
* Build independent implementations
* Develop new approaches

As long as they do not improperly copy protected portions of
the original work.

The purpose of XCSL is to protect original creative effort,
not to block innovation.

---

## Community Over Extraction

The internet has made collaboration easier than ever, but it
has also made exploitation easier than ever.

Projects created for education and communities are sometimes:

* Reuploaded without credit
* Rebranded
* Monetized by third parties
* Locked behind paid systems
* Used commercially without participation from original
  creators

XCSL was designed to discourage extraction-focused behavior
while encouraging contribution-focused behavior.

Communities grow stronger when people:

* Learn honestly
* Give credit fairly
* Share improvements responsibly
* Respect creator boundaries
* Contribute value back into ecosystems

---

## Freedom With Responsibility

Freedom in software communities should come with mutual
respect.

Users should have the freedom to:

* Learn
* Study
* Modify
* Experiment
* Share knowledge

Creators should have the freedom to:

* Receive attribution
* Protect commercial rights
* Define project identity
* Control branding
* Prevent exploitation of their work

XCSL attempts to balance both sides.

---

## Final Philosophy

The purpose of this License is not to prevent learning.

It is not designed to discourage curiosity, experimentation,
research, or collaboration.

Its purpose is to encourage open knowledge sharing while
protecting creators from unfair commercial exploitation,
misrepresentation, and extraction of value without permission.

XCSL believes that communities become healthier when learning
is accessible, contributions remain visible, creators are
respected, and collaboration happens transparently and fairly.
